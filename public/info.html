What we do

There are many ways to create the source file that will be copied or uploaded into Notes Simple. The platform is just expecting a text file. Once that text file is loaded into the system, Notes Simple will do the rest. Our recommendation approach is to record the session, class, or presentation with your phone. Your phone will create a text file of the recording that can be copied into Notes Simple. Many classes also have transcripts which could also be used. Transcripts can be generated automatically if the session is online using a technology like Zoom or MS Team.

How to make a recording in Voice Memos on iPhone

This app can be used to record and share personal notes. Recordings can be edited by trimming, recording over, or replacing parts, adjusting playback speed, removing silence, and improving sound quality. This will create the text file you can upload into Notes Simple.

How to use Live Transcribe with Android

Android devices have many options, so here is one example of using Live Transcribe. You can use Live Transcribe on your Android device to capture speech and sound and see them as text on your screen. You might need to download the app if it is not already on your phone.

How to create transcripts using Zoom

Zoom has live transcription, this feature can be enabled during a meeting by navigating to Settings > Meeting > In Meeting (Advanced), then scrolling down to Automated captions. From there, you can click the blue Enable button, then click the Full transcript toggle button to the right to turn it on. Zoom can also transcribe cloud recordings when enabled in Zoom's settings, this feature automatically transcribes the audio of a meeting or webinar that's recorded to the cloud after the meeting ends. The transcript is saved in VTT format and includes timestamps and speaker identification labels. To enable audio transcription for cloud recordings, you need a Pro, Business, Education, or Enterprise account with cloud recording enabled and account owner or admin privileges.

How to create transcription in Microsoft Teams

Teams live transcripts for meetings with recording and/or transcription are stored in the meeting organizer's OneDrive for Business. The transcript can be accessed through the meeting chat and Recap tab in Teams until a meeting organizer deletes the transcript.
